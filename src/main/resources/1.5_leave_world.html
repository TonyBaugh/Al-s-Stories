A Sudden, Overwhelming Urge – Leave

A weight presses against your chest—expectation, duty, something nameless. But instinct whispers louder.

Leave.

This place, these people, their words—they mean nothing. They feel like they should mean something, but they don’t. Not to you.

You turn. You don’t wait for protests, for explanations, for pleading voices to try and anchor you here. You move.

The moment your foot touches the ground, something shifts.

A ripple moves through the air, subtle yet undeniable, as if the very fabric of this world is reacting to your decision. The murmurs behind you fade into static, blending into the distant hum of the Rift’s presence. You do not look back.

You do not dare.

The landscape before you stretches in ways that do not make sense. A path—was it always there?—winds ahead, vanishing into a horizon that bends and distorts, refusing to remain still. The air is thick with something unseen, something watching.

You keep walking.

The wind shifts, carrying whispers—fragmented, overlapping, urgent. You cannot tell if they are calling you back or urging you forward.

Then, they appear.

Figures step from the ruinous landscape, their cloaks tattered with time, their faces hidden beneath heavy hoods. They do not rush to stop you, nor do they attack. Their presence is both eerie and familiar, as if you have seen them before… somewhere.

“You shouldn’t be here.”

The voice belongs to one of them. Firm, but not hostile. A warning, not a command.

As the group encircles you, a woman steps forward, her piercing silver eyes gleaming beneath her hood. Something about her gaze feels ancient—not in age, but in knowledge.

“If you keep going, you’ll reach the Rift,”

she says.

“But reaching it and leaving are two very different things.”

She studies you, searching for something—perhaps recognition, perhaps understanding—but you offer neither.

After a moment, she tilts her head slightly, as though realizing something.

“…You don’t remember, do you?”

The Rift pulses, deep and resonant. It is waiting. Daring you to choose.

Do you follow the wanderer deeper, trusting that they hold the answers you seek?

Do you confront them, demanding to know what they know before taking another step?

Or do you turn back, unwilling to risk what lies ahead?
