Can a Hero Abandon Their Cause?

The council chambers press in around you—

expectant faces, whispered reassurances,

the weight of leadership settling onto your shoulders

like an iron shroud.

You can see it in their eyes.

They do not need you to remember.

They need you to be what they claim you were.

But you cannot.

“I don’t trust you.”

The words land like a blade driven into stone.

The murmurs falter.

Ilvane’s expression does not change,

but her fingers tighten around the hilt of her sword.

Others exchange uneasy glances,

their confidence cracking like glass.

An elder clears his throat,

voice measured but laced with warning.

“The Rift warps memory,”

he says carefully.

“Perhaps you’ve been gone too long.”

Gone.

The word tugs at something deep within you—

something just out of reach.

It is not memory,

but absence.

A shape where something should be.

Before you can press for answers,

the heavy doors burst open.

A messenger stumbles inside, breathless,

his cloak dusted with the red sands of the outer territories.

He holds a scroll wrapped in black ribbon,

his hands trembling as he extends it toward you.

“This came from the rebels,”

he pants.

“They claim to know the truth.”

A charged silence grips the room.

The elders shift,

their gazes darkening.

The council does not move to stop you,

but their disapproval is a physical thing,

settling like storm clouds over the chamber.

Your fingers brush against the seal,

the wax crumbling at your touch.

You unravel the parchment,

eyes scanning the words scrawled in sharp, deliberate strokes.

And then you freeze.

The Rift.

The war.

The purpose of all of this.

It is nothing like what they told you.

The very foundation of your so-called destiny

may be a lie.
