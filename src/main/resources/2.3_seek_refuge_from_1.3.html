Seek Refuge Away from Conflict

The weight of their expectations clings to you, but you refuse to bear it.

You are not the savior they crave, nor the villain they fear.

Whatever you were before, it no longer defines you.

So, you leave.

The settlement’s outskirts crumble beneath the weight of forgotten time.

Rusted banners, tattered and wind-torn, hang from shattered walls.

The voices behind you fade.

Swallowed by the howling wind.

Streets where children watch in silence.

Eyes tracking your departure, but never calling you back.

They understand.

You will not choose.

The land beyond is a graveyard of forgotten wars.

Ruined outposts rise like broken teeth from the earth, their stones cracked and blackened.

The Rift looms on the horizon, its light casting impossible shapes against the darkening sky.

Even here, you feel it—

Watching.

Waiting.

A ruined chapel stands at the valley’s edge, half-swallowed by time.

Its stained-glass windows are shattered.

Its steeple, twisted by Rift-born scars.

Yet, inside—

The air is still.

Quiet.

For the first time since awakening—

You breathe.

The quiet does not last.

A whisper slithers through the broken walls, threading between shattered pews.

It does not speak in words, but in meaning.

An invitation.

A promise of understanding.

The Rift hums.

Then—

Movement.

A shadow shifts between the ruined pillars.

Not an enemy.

Not a beast.

Something else.

A figure cloaked in tattered robes.

Their presence neither threatening nor welcoming.

They watch you from the dim light filtering through fractured stone.

“Running will not save you,”

they murmur.

“It never has.”

You stiffen.

The Rift pulses.

The whispering wind carries their words deeper into your bones.
