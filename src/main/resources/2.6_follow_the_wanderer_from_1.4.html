They Seem to Recognize You. Should You Trust Them?

The settlement fades behind you, swallowed by the Rift’s unnatural horizon.

The people you left behind—

Their prayers, their warnings, their fear—

They no longer matter.

The only thing that does is the figure walking ahead of you—

Silent.

Sure-footed.

Unburdened by the weight of expectation that clings to your every step.

The wanderer does not speak.

They do not check if you follow.

They already know you will.

There is no true path beneath your feet.

The land shifts—

Slow.

Unsettling.

As if the ground itself is unsure whether it should be stone, sand, or something far older.

You step forward—

For a brief moment, it is like walking through water—

Then solid earth returns beneath your boots.

But now it is cracked.

Glowing veins of violet energy pulse beneath the surface.

The sky darkens.

Not with the coming of night—

But with something else.

Something vast.

Something remembering.

The stars do not remain where they should.

They flicker in and out, shifting.

Constellations forming and unforming—

Like the sky itself is rewriting what it once knew.

The ruins that rise around you are not broken by time.

They are fractured—

Suspended between collapse and rebuilding.

Some pillars stand strong, then vanish.

Others crumble, then reform—

As if their destruction has not yet decided whether it should be real.

The carvings on the walls flicker—

Not like fading paint,

But like words being rewritten—

History shifting as you look at it.

At last, the wanderer speaks.

"This world has already been decided,"

they say.

"And yet, you walk outside of its design."

"Do you know why?"

The question settles into you—

A stone dropped into still water.

You do not answer.

Perhaps you do not know.

Perhaps you do not want to.

The path narrows.

A clearing stretches ahead—

Three roads split before you.

The Rift looms beyond, casting the world in hues of shifting violet and cold silver.

The air itself feels thinner—

Like whatever boundary separates this reality from something else is stretched—

Moments from tearing.

The wanderer finally stops.

They turn toward you for the first time.

Their face is unreadable.

But their eyes—

They do not question.

They do not plead.

They do not hope.

They know.

"You came seeking answers."

"Choose wisely."
