Demanding Answers—At What Cost?

The wanderer lingers at the edge of the gathering, their posture relaxed, unreadable.

Unlike the others, they do not watch you with reverence or fear.

Their expression is one of patience—

As if they knew you would come to them eventually.

But you are not here to follow.

You step forward, closing the distance.

The crowd murmurs, uneasy.

Shifting away from the confrontation—

As if they fear what might come of it.

Even the Rift pulses differently—

Not in warning—

But in anticipation.

"You know something,"

you say, voice steady.

"Tell me."

The wanderer tilts their head.

A breeze stirs—

Though there is no wind.

Shadows lengthen—

Unnaturally.

"I know many things,"

they reply, tone even, unreadable.

"But are you ready for answers?"

You narrow your eyes.

"I don't need riddles. I need truth."

The wanderer exhales a quiet laugh.

Shaking their head.

"Truth is rarely what we want it to be."

The Rift pulses.

Reality shifts.

For a moment, the world flickers—

Not in darkness.

Not in light.

But in absence.

For just a heartbeat—

You are nowhere.

Then—

Everything settles again.

But not quite the same.

The sky is heavier.

The ground beneath your feet—

Colder.

The voices in the distance—

Quieter.

The wanderer studies you.

"You felt it, didn't you?"

"The Rift has already changed you."

"That’s why you can hear it."

The words settle into your mind—

Spreading like cracks in glass.

"Come."

"If you want answers, you will not find them here."

They turn.

Begin to walk.

The others do not stop them.

Do not call after you.

Do not ask what choice you will make.

As if the choice has already been made.

You follow—

Not out of blind trust.

But because the alternative is to stay in the dark.

The world ahead—

Unravels.

The sky no longer holds its shape.

Colors shift—

Deep indigos bleeding into violent crimsons.

Streaks of gold flickering like dying embers.

The air itself—

Too thick.

The ruins—

Not broken by time—

Fractured by something else.

The Wanderer does not slow.

"You are part of something larger than yourself."

"But the Rift is not just power."

"It is not just destruction."

"It is something far older."

"Something that should never have been."

A crossroads looms ahead.

Not roads—

Possibilities.
