Maybe the Only Way Out Is to Deny Everything.

The Rift looms behind you, an impossible storm of color and silence.

The world around you feels fragile,

like a painting smeared before the ink has dried.

You take a step back.

Then another.

Something shifts.

Whatever this place is,

whatever these people expect of you—

it is not yours to claim.

A hush falls over the gathered figures.

Some reach for you—

not to stop you,

but as if they fear what will happen if you leave.

Others avert their eyes,

as if watching would make them complicit.

The Rift pulses.

A tremor through the air,

through your skin,

through the hollow space where your memories should be.

You feel it—

something unraveling.

"I won’t play this game,"

you say, voice sharp.

"I refuse."

A flicker—

something unseen yet felt.

The Rift darkens,

its unnatural hues bleeding into deep obsidian,

streaks of violet curling through the void.

The ground beneath you softens—

no longer solid,

no longer real.

You press forward, determined.

The world twists.

Shapes distort at the edges of your vision.

The sky bends inward,

stars vanishing one by one.

You blink,

and suddenly, the path you walked is gone.

"You shouldn’t have done that."

The voice is not from the crowd.

It is not from the Rift.

It is something else.

A shadow peels itself from the horizon.

Not a person—

not even a shape—

just a void where something should be.

"Where will you go?"

it asks.

You don’t answer.

You don’t know.

The Rift pulses—stronger now,

more insistent.

A cold certainty settles in your bones.

Something has noticed you.

Something does not want you to leave.
