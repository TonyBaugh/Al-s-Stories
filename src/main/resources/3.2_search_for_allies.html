Are You Willing to Risk Unknown Forces for Help?

The figure stands motionless,

their form wavering as though barely tethered to reality.

The Rift pulses behind them,

its warping energy casting jagged, twisting shadows across the battlefield.

"You march toward a war you do not understand,"

they repeat, their voice stretching across time itself.

"And yet you still believe in victory."

A chill curls around your spine.

The soldiers behind you murmur in hushed voices, shifting uneasily.

Ilvane tightens her grip on her sword,

her breath steady but sharp,

like she is forcing herself not to react.

But you?

You step forward.

“If we do not understand,”

you say, voice firm,

“then teach me.”

The figure tilts their head slightly,

the Rift’s glow catching the edges of their shifting armor.

Then, they turn without a word

and begin to walk—

no, drift—

back toward the Rift’s horizon.

The moment you follow,

the world around you twists.

The battlefield vanishes.

The air grows heavy, thick with something beyond breath—

something vast, something watching.

The sky overhead fractures like shattered glass,

stars peeking through the splintered void where they should not be.

A sprawling structure emerges before you,

formed from Riftstone—

its walls shimmer between ancient, crumbling ruins

and something newly built,

something shifting between states of existence.

The Archive of the Riftborn.

A place forgotten by time.

Or perhaps erased from it.

The Riftborn Scholars move like ghosts through its halls,

their robes woven with threads of shifting light,

their faces concealed beneath hoods that seem to fold inward, endless.

They do not speak immediately,

but they acknowledge your presence,

a silent recognition of something inevitable.

"You do not want power,"

one scholar finally murmurs,

their voice echoing softly in the vast silence.

"You want to know."

They lead you deeper.

Towering shelves stretch beyond sight,

books bound in dark leather inscribed with symbols

that twist the longer you stare at them.

Scrolls float between hands that never touch them,

their words flickering between languages long dead

and ones yet to be spoken.

One of the scholars halts before a great obsidian pedestal.

A tome rests upon it, bound not in paper,

but in something that ripples like liquid void.

"This was not written,"

the scholar whispers, reverence lacing their voice.

"It was remembered."

Your fingers brush the cover,

and the Rift hums.

The moment you open the tome,

the world shifts.

The First Revelation.

A time before war.

Before suffering.

A civilization that did not fear the Rift,

but understood it.

Thrived within it.

The First Disappearance.

An age swallowed in silence.

A history rewritten, not by time,

but by design.

The Final Act.

The Rift was never an accident.

It was never a wound.

It was a choice.

"You were not meant to see this."

The scholar at your side exhales shakily.

Their hands tremble as they close the book,

their hood tilting slightly toward you.

"The Rift is not merely a fracture in reality,"

they say.

"It is a door.

And someone—

something—

has been deciding what remains on either side."

Beneath your feet, the ground shudders.

The realization settles into your bones,

deeper than fear.

This is not lost history.

This is erased history.

And if it was erased once…

it can be erased again.

The Rift hums—

not in warning,

but in acknowledgment.

It knows you see it now.

And now,

it waits for your next move.
