If It Is a Wound in Reality, Can It Be Closed?

The Rift looms ahead,

a storm of impossible hues—

violet folding into gold,

obsidian streaked with pulses of searing white.

Its presence is overwhelming,

its pulse thrumming through the ground,

through your bones,

through the space behind your eyes.

It is alive.

And it is waiting.

Behind you,

your path is already fading.

Whether you marched here as a warrior,

sought knowledge as a scholar,

or fled to escape the war that raged beyond its horizon,

none of it matters now.

All roads lead to the Rift.

The council’s voices echo in your memory—

warnings, desperate prayers,

half-formed theories of what the Rift is

and what it can become.

No one has ever sealed it before.

No one has ever dared.

But they were not you.

Scholars stand at the edge of the anomaly,

their hands moving feverishly over arcane symbols

etched into the shattered remains of the land.

They draw sigils in luminous ink,

etch equations into stone

that no longer obeys physics.

“The Rift is not merely a tear in reality,”

one of them murmurs,

their voice hoarse from sleepless nights.

“It is woven through everything.

If we close it,

we close something far greater than we understand.”

You press a hand against the shifting air near the Rift’s core.

It is warm.

Not like fire,

not like flesh.

Like something vast.

Something aware.

It pushes back against you.

Slow at first,

then with more force.

It does not want to be sealed.

A deep vibration rattles through the earth.

The symbols glow brighter,

lines of energy snapping like taut strings drawn too tight.

The Rift is fighting back.

And then—

a voice.

It does not come from the scholars.

It does not come from your soldiers.

“You cannot close what was never meant to be open.”

The Rift flickers,

its swirling mass of light distorting

into something more familiar,

more human—

a silhouette,

just at the edge of understanding.

Their form is indistinct,

shifting with the Rift’s ever-changing pulse.

“Do you even know what you are trying to destroy?”

The ground beneath you fractures,

and suddenly,

sealing the Rift no longer feels

like an act of salvation.

It feels like an execution.
