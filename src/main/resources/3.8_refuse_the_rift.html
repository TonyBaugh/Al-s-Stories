Is Resistance Even Possible?

The Rift looms,

an ever-pulsing wound in the fabric of existence,

its glow pressing against your skin

like static before a storm.

You feel it reaching,

threading into the spaces between your thoughts,

urging you forward.

The air does not move,

yet something unseen tugs at you,

whispering in a voice both foreign and familiar.

But you do not step closer.

You plant your feet

against the shifting, unsteady ground,

resisting the pull that has claimed so many before you.

You refuse.

A hush falls over the world.

The Rift pulses—

once,

twice—

before something in it stutters.

The swirling colors slow,

flickering uncertainly,

as though it had never truly considered defiance.

The sky fractures.

The landscape around you flickers,

as if the world itself is adjusting,

recalibrating,

trying to erase an anomaly—

you.

Shadows stretch impossibly long,

clawing toward the Rift,

dragging against the ground like grasping hands.

"You reject it?"

A voice,

layered and discordant,

echoes from the abyss.

The Rift swells,

its glow deepening into something no human tongue could describe.

"You do not refuse the inevitable.

You delay it."

The ground beneath you lurches violently,

and a sharp pain lances through your mind—

memories not your own,

countless lives,

countless refusals,

all swallowed by the same fate.

You see yourself,

standing in this very place,

over and over again.

In some visions,

you run.

In others,

you kneel.

In many,

you are dragged forward,

screaming.

But never—

not once—

have you stood your ground.

The Rift remembers.

A gust of wind howls through the void,

but it carries no sound,

no scent—

only weight.

The weight of every choice made before.

The weight of what comes next.

And then,

the Rift does something

it has never done before.

It waits.

No voices.

No whispers.

No visions clawing at your mind.

Just the unbearable,

expectant silence.

As if it is seeing,

truly seeing,

for the first time…

whether you will break.
