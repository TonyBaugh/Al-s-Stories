The Rift Itself Determines the Final Fate

The Rift has been pulling at you since the beginning. Whispering. Beckoning. But now, as you stand at the threshold of its ever-shifting abyss, you do something no one has dared before.

You do not resist it.

You do not accept it.

You do not reject it.

You let it decide.

The moment you surrender your will to the Rift—not as prey, not as conqueror, but as an equal—the world shudders. The weight of reality buckles, the ground beneath you fracturing into swirling threads of violet and obsidian. The air trembles with an unnatural stillness, as if the very laws of existence hesitate, unsure of how to proceed.

The Rift pulses—once, twice—then slows, as if considering.

For the first time, it does not lash out. It does not consume. It does not pull you into its depths. It watches.

And then, it reaches.

Your mind is flooded with sensation—not words, not images, but possibilities. Futures unravel and reweave themselves in rapid succession. A thousand versions of yourself flicker through your vision—some standing as rulers, some kneeling as sacrifices, some dissolving into the Rift itself, becoming something other.

It has always been like this.

It has never been like this.

You have always been here.

You were never meant to be here.

The Rift is showing you everything. Every iteration. Every mistake. Every moment of choice and consequence, folded atop one another like the pages of a book too heavy to read in one lifetime.

And now, it turns the final page.

You feel it shift, settling upon a path that was always waiting, always watching, always shaping itself in response to you.

A presence brushes against your thoughts—immense, unfathomable. Not cruel, not kind. Simply aware.

You are not in control anymore.

You never were.

The Rift decides.

A final pulse erupts outward—not of destruction, not of chaos, but of understanding. You are left with a choice that is not yours to make alone. The Rift has spoken. It has chosen for you.

It offers two paths—both endings to this story, both inevitable in their own way.

One is an ending of sacrifice. A way to restore what has been broken, but at the cost of yourself. The Rift does not grant mercy, but it understands necessity. To become part of it is to hold reality together—to give yourself so that others may live.

The other is an ending beyond endings. A path beyond the Rift itself. To step past the boundary of existence, to leave behind everything and move into the unknown. The Rift has chosen you for this, for something greater. But what lies beyond? Even it does not know.
