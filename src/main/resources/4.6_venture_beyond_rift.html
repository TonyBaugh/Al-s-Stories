The Unknown Calls—Will You Step Through?

The Rift stretches before you, no longer just a wound in the world but a vast, infinite expanse.

It does not churn like a storm.

It does not hunger.

It waits.

Deep violet, endless midnight,

streaks of gold twisting through an abyss of nothing.

The closer you stand, the more you feel it.

Not a pull—

a choice.

The ground beneath your feet fractures.

Crystalline shards form at the edges,

reality fraying against something greater.

The air hums—not in warning, but in invitation.

"You are not the first to stand here."

The words do not come from behind you.

They do not come from the Rift.

They bloom inside your mind, threading through your thoughts,

as if the Rift itself is speaking through you.

You glance back.

The world behind you—

the ruins, the choices, the echoes of who you were—

blur at the edges.

Already fading.

Beyond the Rift, shapes shift.

Figures—

distorted reflections—

echoes of those who stepped forward before you.

Some dissolve instantly, becoming light, becoming something else.

Others hesitate.

They flicker—between existence and erasure—before vanishing.

Is this what happens to those who seek too much?

A breath.

A choice.

Step forward, and the Rift will take you.

It will change you.

Perhaps into something new.

Perhaps into nothing at all.

Or resist—

And perhaps, for the first time, force the Rift to answer.

The Rift pulses, slow and deliberate.

It is waiting.
