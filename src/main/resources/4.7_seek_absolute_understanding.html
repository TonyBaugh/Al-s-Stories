If Knowledge is Power, Will You Control It or Succumb?

The Rift is no longer a fracture. No longer a tear. No longer broken.

It breathes. It watches. It waits.

Its glow does not pull, does not demand.

It simply knows.

You step forward.

The shifting hues within the Rift deepen—

golden threads woven through the abyss.

A structure beneath the chaos.

A pattern too vast for the human mind to grasp.

But you try.

Your fingers tingle as you reach out.

The Rift does not recoil.

It does not strike.

It does not resist.

It allows.

And the moment you touch it—

You see.

The past.

The future.

The uncountable loops of history converging into something singular.

Echoes of yourself, stretching across infinity.

You see your reflection in them, but it is not you.

It is something greater.

Something beyond.

The Rift opens.

And the knowledge floods in.

Not a trickle.

Not a stream.

An ocean, an endless, all-consuming tide.

You are drowning.

Or perhaps—

You are becoming.

A voice—distant, familiar.

"Stop!"

The world outside is slipping.

Ilvane’s voice reaches you, sharp, panicked.

But she is already too far away.

The Rift hums.

It does not care for Ilvane.

It does not care for war.

It does not care for you.

It only offers.

And it will give you everything.

But can you hold onto it?

The stars inside the Rift flicker.

Time fractures.

Reality bends.

The choice is yours.
