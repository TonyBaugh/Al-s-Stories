Lead Your People to War

The war council gathers in the great hall, a fortress of stone and steel that has withstood both time and siege.

The air reeks of oil and embers, torches lining the chamber casting jagged shadows against banners torn and battle-worn. The table before you is a battlefield of its own—maps inked with blood-red lines, markers placed with the weight of lives yet to be lost. The room thrums with expectation, with hunger.

Every eye is on you.

"You’ve seen what they do."

The commander’s gauntlet slams onto the table, steel screeching against the wood, leaving deep gouges. "How many more must we lose before we strike first?"

A murmur spreads through the chamber like wildfire, sparking in the cold steel of their stares. These are not men and women bound by duty alone. They are here because they crave an answer—one only you can provide.

Some seek vengeance, others survival, but all of them have chosen you as their spearhead. Their general. Their ruler.

Beyond the heavy iron doors of the war hall, the world trembles beneath the Rift’s ceaseless pulse. Its glow seeps through the cracks in the stone walls, bleeding hues of shifting indigo, deep crimson, and eerie violet across the chamber.

The Rift does not rest. It does not forget.

It has given power. It has taken life. And now, it will be the battlefield upon which you carve your legend into eternity.

At the far end of the room, Ilvane stands, arms folded, her silver-streaked hair catching the flickering torchlight. Her gaze is a dagger, unreadable and piercing. She does not fidget, does not speak out of turn. But when she finally does, her voice is as precise as the blade at her hip.

“We march to claim what is ours.”

Measured. Unwavering.

"But this war is not just steel and blood. The Rift itself is in play. If we move, we must be prepared for the impossible."

Her words coil around you, planting something deeper than strategy—doubt? No. Something colder.

Awareness.

This war will not be won by numbers alone. The Rift is not just an obstacle, nor a passive force in the chaos to come. It is waiting. It is watching.

Your soldiers—your people—watch as well. Their faces lined with the weight of war, of history rewritten by those too weak to hold the pen.

But you are no scribe.

You are no mere player in this game.

You are the one who shapes the board itself.

And yet, how you take control will define everything.
