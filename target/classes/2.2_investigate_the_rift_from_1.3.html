Investigate the Rift’s Nature

The council chambers buzz with cautious deliberation.

Maps, records, and fragmented accounts litter the great stone table before you.

They speak of war, of seizing power, of striking first before the unknown strikes them.

But you refuse to be bound by their fears.

Too many have carved their graves into the battlefield without ever understanding what they fought against.

“I will not command an army toward an enemy we do not understand.”

Your words land like iron.

The Rift is not simply a battlefield.

It is something more.

A hush follows.

Some look away, unwilling to challenge you.

Others glare, distrust flickering behind their eyes.

Then—

“You are not the first to seek answers.”

The elder seated near the fire does not raise his head.

His hands, weathered with age and ink, tighten against the table.

“But the Rift does not give knowledge freely. It demands something in return.”

The archives beneath the stronghold are cold, damp with forgotten time.

Remnants of a lost past rest in fragile pages and dust-choked tomes.

The scholars who dwell here are not warriors.

Their hands are ink-stained, their eyes shadowed by truths no blade can cut through.

They whisper of civilizations lost.

Of kings and scholars who sought the Rift’s secrets—

Only to be erased from history.

There are maps drawn with trembling hands, marked with fractures in reality.

Records of symbols—glyphs carved at the heart of the Rift.

Symbols that belong to no known language.

And then—

There is the book.

Bound in dark leather, cracked with age.

It sits untouched at the far end of the chamber.

The scholars do not approach it.

They do not speak of it.

But when your fingers brush its cover—

You swear you feel a heartbeat.

One of the scholars steps forward.

His voice barely above a whisper.

“This was not written.”

He swallows.

“It was remembered.”

You open it.

The ink does not sit upon the page.

It moves.

Living, shifting.

The words do not wait for you to read them.

They form, as if responding to your presence.

The first Rift was not an accident.

It was a choice.

A chill coils down your spine.

You turn the page.

More words bleed into existence.

The Rift was never meant to be contained.

Only understood.

The air shifts.

The candlelight flickers.

The walls groan, as if bending under an unseen weight.

A hum.

Distant—yet impossibly close.

The Rift is listening.
