What Happens if You Do Nothing?

The Rift looms behind you,

a wound in the sky that refuses to close.

The people who watch you expect a choice,

a declaration, an answer.

But you give them none.

You turn and walk away.

Your departure is not met with protests—

only silence.

Some watch with quiet disappointment,

others with confusion.

But a few... a few seem to understand.

Not everyone is meant for war.

Not every path is forged in battle.

The land beyond the settlement is broken and restless,

shaped by forces beyond comprehension.

The sky is a swirling gradient of deep violet and ember gold,

clouds drifting too slowly,

as if caught between moments of time.

The ground beneath your feet is uneven—

crimson sands shifting over what once might have been a road,

now long consumed by entropy.

In the distance, skeletal remains of ancient towers rise

like the ribs of some long-dead beast,

their structures cracked and half-sunken into the earth.

You do not know how long you walk

before you see it—

a sanctuary, untouched by war.

Nestled between fractured hills and jagged spires of obsidian,

the hidden refuge glows with lantern light.

Structures of stone and Rift-tempered glass stand defiantly against time,

woven through with sprawling vines that pulse with faint luminescence.

The air is thick with the scent of something unfamiliar—

not decay,

but something alive,

something ancient.

A cloaked figure waits at the entrance,

their face hidden beneath the deep hood of a traveler.

When they speak, their voice is calm, measured.

"You do not seek war,"

they say.

"You do not seek power.

But what is it you do seek?"

You hesitate.

The question is not an accusation,

nor a challenge.

It is an invitation.

Here, away from the war camps and the scholars

who whisper of truths beyond comprehension,

another path exists.

A path away from war.

A path away from the Rift.

But is that truly possible?

You step forward,

the weight of decision settling over you once more.

The sanctuary is open to you,

but the choice is yours.
