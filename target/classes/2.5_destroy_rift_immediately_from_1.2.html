Destroy the Rift Immediately

The Rift looms before you, an open wound festering at the heart of the world.

It is an insult to order, an affront to dominion. It resists control, distorts reality, and defies logic.

But all things—no matter how ancient, no matter how untamed—can be destroyed.

And destruction is your specialty.

The war room is alight with debate. Commanders stand around the great iron table, maps of the fractured world sprawled before them.

Thick strokes of red ink mark Rift anomalies—wounds in the land that spread like an infection. Some fear it, others seek to exploit it.

You see only a threat that must be erased.

“The Rift is a parasite,”

you say, voice cutting through hesitation.

“And parasites can be excised.”

A veteran commander folds her arms, steel glinting beneath the scars.

“And if we fail? If it consumes us before we can strike?”

You meet her gaze, unyielding.

“If we fail, then we were never meant to win.”

Your forces assemble before the Rift, its monstrous radiance pulsing, watching, waiting.

Weapons forged from shattered civilizations gleam in the Rift’s unnatural twilight. Siege engines line the valley, their presence a grim promise.

This is not a battle.

This is an execution.

A signal flare erupts—a streak of violet and red—and the first wave of fire rains down.

Bolts of Rift-tempered steel pierce its glow, vanishing into the abyss like stones swallowed by an ocean.

Explosions ripple across the horizon—

—but the Rift does not shrink.

It does not waver.

It reacts.

A deafening pulse shatters the air, a shockwave ripping through the battlefield with the force of a hurricane.

Soldiers are flung backward, the earth fracturing beneath them. The Rift’s glow darkens—

—not retreating—

—but devouring.

You stand firm. But as the wind howls around you, your mind races.

The Rift is not dying.

It is responding.

And in its response, you feel something far worse than defiance.

You feel recognition.

For a moment—

The world vanishes.

You stand at the edge of something infinite, something vast and knowing.

You see glimpses—

Flashes of history torn apart and rewritten.

Echoes of battles fought in cycles beyond comprehension.

Your own face—

Staring back at you—

From countless reflections.

Versions of yourself that were.

That could have been.

That never were.
