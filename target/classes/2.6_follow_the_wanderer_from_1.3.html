Follow the Wanderer into the Unknown

The Rift pulses behind you, a heartbeat in the fabric of reality.

But you turn away.

From the voices that call you hero.

From the ones who brand you villain.

From those who claim to know who you are.

Instead, you follow the one figure who does not beg.

Does not command.

Does not fear.

The Wanderer.

They do not look back.

They do not ask if you will keep pace.

This is not a test of obedience.

This is a test of will.

You chose this path. Now, you must walk it.

The ruins stretch before you, remnants of a city long devoured by time.

Twisted spires rise toward the Rift-lit sky, their forms warped by forces unseen.

The air thickens with whispers—

They do not form words, yet you understand them.

The Wanderer does not slow.

At last, they stop.

A sunken courtyard lies before you, its earth split apart, revealing layers of forgotten history.

Tattered banners hang from broken archways.

Their symbols lost.

Their meaning forgotten.

At the center of the ruins stands a great stone monolith, its carvings shifting as you watch.

Never the same twice.

The Wanderer turns at last.

Their hood slips, just enough for you to see their eyes—

Ancient.

Knowing.

Reflecting something vast and distant.

“You are not the first to walk this path,”

they murmur.

“And you will not be the last.”

The words settle into your bones.

Not a warning.

A certainty.

You open your mouth to speak—to demand answers—

To ask why they led you here—

But the ground beneath you pulses.

Not just the earth—

The air.

The stone.

The monolith’s carvings flicker, shifting too fast to follow.

Wars.

Betrayals.

Figures that look like you—

But are not you.

Fractured echoes of a past you do not recall.

The Wanderer watches.

Unreadable.

“You came seeking answers,”

they say.

“But will you listen when you hear them?”

The air shudders.

The Rift hums.
