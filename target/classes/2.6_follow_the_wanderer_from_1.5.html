They Offer an Escape—But at What Cost?

Something about this place—these people, their stories—

feels wrong.

The weight of expectation presses against your chest, suffocating.

Like a script you were never meant to follow.

But the Wanderer is different.

They do not plead for your loyalty.

They do not demand answers.

They only wait.

Their cloak is tattered at the edges,

dark fabric shifting like liquid shadow,

as if the Rift’s energy clings to them.

Their expression is impossible to read—

their eyes holding something far older

than the people behind you.

"You don’t belong here,"

they say, their voice carrying over the Rift’s hum.

"You feel it, don’t you?"

You do.

A sensation that gnaws at the edges of your mind,

whispering of a truth you cannot quite grasp.

"Come with me,"

the Wanderer continues.

"Before it’s too late."

Behind you, murmurs rise.

Uncertainty.

Some whisper your name like a prayer;

others look away, unwilling to acknowledge what is happening.

As if they have seen it before.

You step forward.

The Rift shudders.

The Wanderer does not wait for further hesitation.

They turn—

moving past the settlement’s crumbling walls,

stepping into the ruins beyond.

And you follow—

not because you trust them,

but because something deep inside you knows

that staying is the greater risk.

The ruins stretch endlessly before you,

their forms shifting, flickering between

the remnants of a forgotten civilization

and something far stranger.

Walls rise and fall,

structures twisting,

as if time itself is trying to remember

what this place was supposed to be.

The sky above fractures with violet streaks,

the air thick with static,

heavy with something just beyond comprehension.

"This place existed before the Rift was ever named,"

the Wanderer says,

their steps light over ground that barely seems real.

"But that doesn’t mean it was ever meant to exist."

They lead you deeper.

Past broken pillars where inscriptions have been worn away—

not by time,

but by something deliberate.

Past monuments that seem half-formed,

figures carved into them flickering between forms,

as if their very identities were rewritten.

"You wanted to leave,"

the Wanderer murmurs.

"But do you even know where you would go?"

The question lingers.

Because you don’t.

Because maybe the world beyond this place—

the one you long to escape to—

doesn’t exist anymore.

The Wanderer slows as the path opens ahead—

a shattered bridge leading toward a vast, spiraling emptiness.

The Rift looms beyond it,

impossibly large,

its energy not just a tear in the world,

but a wound that never healed.

"If you still wish to leave,"

they say,

"then cross the bridge."

"But understand this—"

they turn to face you,

expression unreadable,

but voice heavy with certainty.

"Once you step beyond this world,

there is no coming back."

The Rift pulses—watching.

The Rift waits.
