What Do They Know That You Don’t?

The Rift pulses behind you, an ever-present force pressing against reality itself.

The crowd watches—some in silent reverence, others in fear—

but none move to stop you.

They know better.

The Wanderer stands at the edge of the gathering, waiting.

Their cloak, dark and frayed, shifts unnaturally in the Rift’s glow.

Their presence is not like the others—

it does not belong,

but neither does it defy.

They watch without expectation, without judgment.

Without fear.

You step forward.

"Who are you?"

The demand cuts through the air like a blade.

The Wanderer does not flinch.

"Who are you?"

they return, voice quiet, yet heavy with meaning.

The Rift pulses—sharper this time.

The wind shifts, carrying whispers not your own.

"I don’t care about riddles,"

you press.

"You know something. Speak plainly."

The Wanderer studies you.

Then, they exhale—

a slow, measured breath.

Not in frustration,

but acceptance.

"Very well."

They raise a hand.

The Rift pulses in response.

The crowd behind you shifts uneasily.

"You don’t belong here."

The words strike deep—

not an accusation,

not a prophecy,

but a truth.

The Rift flickers at the edges of your vision.

The world itself feels thinner, stretched—

as if your very presence is disrupting something.

"And yet,"

the Wanderer continues, stepping closer,

"here you stand."

A question with no answer.

A fate already decided.

Their silver eyes bore into yours, something ancient behind them.

"You could leave,"

they offer.

"Turn away from this place, from them, from all of it.

Walk into the Rift and never return."

Behind them, the Rift swirls,

its impossible hues flickering like a living thing.

"Or you could stay,"

the Wanderer murmurs.

"And face what you already suspect is true."

The crowd holds its breath.

Even the Rift seems to wait.
