What If the Truth Is Worse Than the War?

The air here is thick—

with the scent of ink,

of burning sage,

a strange mixture of wisdom and warning.

The walls of the archive stretch beyond sight,

twisting in impossible spirals,

shelves stacked with books that do not gather dust,

but pulse—

as if alive.

Knowledge lingers here—

not abandoned,

not forgotten,

but waiting.

The Rift’s light filters through

arched windows of shattered glass,

casting shifting hues of violet,

crimson, and gold

across the stone floor.

The scholars move like specters among the records—

hooded figures with ink-stained hands,

whispering in languages not meant for human tongues.

They do not bow.

They do not acknowledge you.

At least, not in the way the others did.

One of them finally turns—

a woman draped in violet robes,

the Rift’s glow caught in the silver threads

woven into her sleeves.

Her expression is unreadable,

but her voice—

"You are too late."

The words send a chill down your spine.

"The Rift is not something to be understood—

it is something that has already understood us."

She leads you deeper,

past halls where books are not read,

but deciphered—

where scholars transcribe prophecies older than memory,

their fingers trembling

as ink bleeds onto parchment.

They do not speak to you.

Perhaps they do not see you.

Or perhaps—

they see too much.

She stops at a towering obsidian door,

its surface etched with shifting symbols—

letters that flicker,

changing even as you try to focus.

Her hand hovers over it, hesitant,

as if she is about to unmake something

simply by acknowledging it.

"This was not written," she whispers.

"It was remembered."

The door opens without touch.

Inside—

the room is not a room at all.

It is a void.

A space between moments,

between thoughts,

between realities.

Floating before you,

bound in chains of shifting light,

is something that should not exist—

a book with pages woven from starlight,

a tome that hums with the same pulse

as the Rift itself.

You take a step forward.

The Rift pulses in response.

A voice—

**not the scholar’s, not your own—**

resonates through the chamber,

vibrating in your bones.

"If you open this,

you will see what must not be seen."

Your breath catches.

The Rift watches.

Waits.
