It Calls to You. What Will You Become?

The Rift is no longer just a tear in reality—

it is a presence.

It pulses, slow and steady,

like the heartbeat of something immense

and unseen.

The world around you is shifting,

bending under its influence—

colors bleed into one another,

the sky warps between endless night

and blinding dawn,

the ground beneath your feet

twists in ways that defy all logic.

You should be afraid.

You should turn back.

But you don’t.

Because despite everything—

the warnings,

the horrors,

the whispers of those lost to its depths—

the Rift is calling to you.

Each step forward feels lighter, easier,

as though the Rift is drawing you in,

removing all resistance.

Shadows stretch and twist along the landscape,

forming shapes—figures,

familiar and unfamiliar,

shifting through the haze of time.

They are not enemies.

They are echoes.

Memories not just of the past,

but of every possibility,

every version of yourself that could have existed.

And they are waiting.

"You have come far."

The voice is not spoken aloud,

nor does it come from any single source.

It resonates from the Rift itself,

from the space between thoughts,

the silence between heartbeats.

It is not demanding.

It is not forceful.

It simply… is.

A figure emerges from the swirling light—

a form half-shaped,

flickering between human and something greater,

their features indistinct,

shifting between too many possibilities at once.

They watch you

with eyes that do not exist,

yet you feel their gaze settle deep

into your very being.

"This is what you were meant for."

The air around you crackles,

charged with energy,

humming in perfect synchronization with your pulse.

The Rift is no longer something external.

It is inside of you,

something that has always been there,

waiting to be acknowledged.

A thousand voices whisper in unison—

not urging,

not demanding—

only offering.

You can feel it—

the choice settling into your bones.

To accept the Rift fully

is to become something new,

to step beyond the boundaries of what you were

and embrace what you could be.

To resist, to fight its pull,

is to defy a force greater than anything mortal—

but perhaps,

that resistance is what makes you human.

Or, perhaps,

there is a third path.

To listen,

to understand,

to question what even the Rift itself

may not know.

The shifting form before you

reaches out a hand—

not commanding,

but inviting.

The Rift pulses,

waiting.
