If This World is Doomed, Can You Leave?

The Rift stretches before you—

a wound carved into the fabric of existence,

bleeding light and shadow in equal measure.

You should not be here.

Every fiber of your being tells you this.

The Rift is not meant to be touched,

not meant to be understood.

It is the unknown made manifest,

the abyss given form.

And yet,

you stand at its edge.

You do not look back.

Whether you came here fleeing from war,

from destiny,

or from the weight of a world that no longer feels real,

it no longer matters.

The Rift is an exit,

a threshold between what is

and what could be.

A doorway without a destination.

And you are about to step through.

The air crackles, charged with colors that do not exist,

hues that have no name.

The world behind you—

the ruins,

the people,

the fading echoes of your past—

seems distant now,

like something half-remembered from a dream.

You take a breath.

Then another.

And you step forward.

The Rift swallows you whole.

There is no ground beneath your feet.

No sky above your head.

There is only motion—

free-falling through a space that bends and twists,

an ever-expanding spiral of shattered possibilities.

You feel yourself stretching,

splitting into echoes,

into fragments—

each version of you flickering across infinity.

Somewhere,

you stand at the edge of the Rift,

never stepping through.

Somewhere,

you fell long ago,

your name already forgotten.

Somewhere,

you never existed at all.

And yet,

you remain.

Something tethers you—

a single thread

in the unraveling weave of existence.

You can feel it pulling,

straining against the endless drift.

A choice must be made.

Ahead,

a flickering passage—

a tunnel of fractured light and liquid shadow,

twisting, shifting, waiting.

A way forward.

A way out.

But below,

the Rift stretches wider,

a yawning abyss

that offers something else entirely.

The whispers return,

soft this time,

inviting.

The Rift is not just a door.

It is something deeper.

Something aware.

And it is watching you.

You are not the first to come this way.

You will not be the last.

The Rift shudders,

waiting for your decision.
