4:2 Resist the Rift’s Pull

The Rift looms before you, a cosmic wound in reality, pulsing in slow, deliberate waves. The air around it shudders, warping in time with its shifting hues—deep violets collapsing into abyssal black, streaks of gold flickering like dying stars.

Its presence claws at the edges of your perception, a hum beneath your skin that has grown stronger, threading through your veins like a second heartbeat—persistent, insistent. It does not call you. It demands you.

You plant your feet, forcing yourself to resist the pull. The weight of expectation, of fate, of something far older than the war you came here to fight, bears down on you.

“Whatever it’s doing to you, fight it.”

Ilvane’s voice slices through the thick, suffocating air, but you barely hear her. The Rift speaks in a language deeper than words, a whisper carved into the marrow of your bones. It is not merely a force—it is something aware. Something waiting.

Then, the vision strikes.

A battlefield stretching beyond time, layered with echoes of this moment playing out over and over. A thousand selves. A thousand choices. Some march forward into the Rift and vanish. Some kneel before it and rise changed. Others—too few—turn away, only to be dragged back.

One truth binds them all—none have resisted.

Until now.

You tighten your grip on reality, on yourself. The Rift shudders. It does not like this answer. It does not understand refusal.

The ground beneath you fractures, threads of deep crimson light spilling through the cracks. A soundless scream erupts from the void, and then—they emerge.

Shadowed figures, half-formed echoes of those who came before. Their movements are jagged, unnatural, as if reality itself is unsure whether they should exist. Hollow eyes, swirling with violet flame, fix on you with a hunger that is not their own.

These are not the fallen.

These are the ones who failed to resist.

A tremor runs through you—not of fear, but of resolve. You will not become them.

The Rift senses your defiance. The air quakes with its fury, jagged tendrils of energy lashing out, desperate to pull you in. The weight of it is unbearable—like trying to hold back an ocean with bare hands.

And yet, something shifts.

The Rift wavers, flickers. The voices that have haunted you since this journey began hesitate. They are not used to rejection.

For the first time, the Rift feels something new.

Doubt.
