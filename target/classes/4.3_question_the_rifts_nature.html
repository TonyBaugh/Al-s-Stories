If Knowledge is Power, What Happens When You Know Too Much?

The war, the alliances, the desperate struggles for control—none of it mattered.

Every path, every choice, every step has led you here,

to the Rift’s threshold,

where knowledge looms like a storm waiting to break.

And yet, you hesitate.

The air around the Rift is heavier than it should be,

pressing against your skin like unseen hands,

wrapping around your limbs like a tide eager to pull you in.

Each breath is thick with static, tinged with something ancient,

something waiting.

The Rift is not just a force—

it is aware.

The flickering swirls of violet and silver in its depths

are not random, not chaotic.

They are moving in patterns.

A realization takes root, cold and undeniable.

The Rift is not a wound.

It was never broken.

It was built.

It was placed.

And it was placed for a reason.

"You shouldn’t have come here."

The voice rasps from the shadows of the Rift’s glow,

and a figure steps forward.

Their form flickers,

shifting at the edges,

as though reality itself is unsure

whether they should exist.

Their robes are ancient,

their face obscured—

not by a hood,

but by something far more unnatural.

A blur,

a distortion,

a smudging of existence itself.

"You weren’t supposed to see."

The Rift hums, deep and resonant.

It is no longer a presence in the distance—

it is here,

within you,

around you,

a pulse synchronizing with your own heartbeat.

Then, the weight presses into your mind.

Not an attack.

Not a demand.

An offer.

A glimpse.

A memory.

A truth.
