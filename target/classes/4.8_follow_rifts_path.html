Escape Beckons, but is it Real?

The Rift pulses in slow, deliberate waves, a rhythm that now matches your own breath.

It does not threaten.

It does not call.

It simply exists.

The battlefield. The ruins. The shifting sands of reality.

They are smearing.

Colors bleed together, twisting into something beyond understanding.

The voices of those who warned you, who begged you to turn back, become nothing more than whispers—

Distant. Fading. Lost.

And then—

A path unfolds before you.

Not a road.

Not a corridor.

Something fluid.

Something waiting.

"Where do you think that leads?"

Ilvane’s voice reaches you, sharp with concern.

You do not answer.

You do not know.

But something in your bones does.

Beyond this, beyond the Rift, there is something else.

Something older.

Something real.

Something waiting.

Your fingers graze the Rift’s edge.

It is cool, like the surface of still water.

For the first time, you wonder—

Is this the only way forward?

Maybe the world you came from was never the only world at all.

Behind you, the last remnants of what you fought for flicker—

Unstable.

The weight of expectation, of destiny, of history itself presses against your back.

Urging you to stay.

To fight.

To resist.

But another part of you—the part that has always questioned—

Wonders if any of it was ever real to begin with.

A final step.

The Rift trembles—not in warning, but in recognition.

You can leave.

You can go where none before you have dared.

But can you truly escape the Rift?

Or are you only walking deeper into it?

The answer is just one step away.
