Destruction: Oblivion’s Reckoning

The Rift convulses, a maelstrom of swirling violet and obsidian, its form warping, twisting, collapsing in on itself like a dying star. The sky fractures above, veins of gold and crimson searing through the darkness, burning away the remnants of what once was. The ground beneath you heaves, trembling as though the world itself is recoiling from what is about to happen.

But you do not falter.

You deny the Rift.

You reject its existence.

You end it.

A force erupts from your being, not of the Rift’s making, not of some unknowable power—it is yours. Your will becomes a blinding storm of pure unmaking, a force so absolute that the Rift, for all its vastness, its eternity, its unknowable depths, begins to shatter.

It screams.

Not in sound. Not in words. But in the very fabric of reality, a desperate, violent thrashing as it fights against the inevitable. Tendrils of energy lash out, wild and erratic, trying to find something—anything—to hold onto.

But there is nothing left for it to grasp.

You burn it away.

The world around you unravels, crumbling into motes of silver dust as the Rift collapses further, its great wound upon existence sealing not with careful precision, but with raw, final destruction. The Rift was never meant to be sealed. It was never meant to be contained.

It was meant to be ended.

And so, you do.

The last remnants of the Rift detonate outward, a shockwave of finality that ripples across the very bones of reality. The sky—once torn and bleeding—turns black. The land—once twisted and warped—splinters into nothingness. The very concept of the Rift, of its power, of everything it had ever touched—is erased.

And then… silence.

A silence so deep, so profound, that for a moment, it feels as though nothing will ever exist again.

You stand upon the ruins of what once was. But there are no ruins, are there? No remnants, no scars.

Only emptiness.

You look beyond the horizon, but there is no horizon left.

You look toward the sky, but the sky has been wiped clean.

You look down at your hands, and for the first time, they do not tremble with power.

Because there is nothing left to fight.

Nothing left to destroy.

Nothing left at all.

Summary – Destruction: Oblivion’s Reckoning

The Rift has been erased—not just destroyed, but completely removed from existence. Its power, its corruption, its very presence has been unmade.

But so has everything else.

The world that warred over it, the knowledge it held, the echoes of all who ever touched it—all of it is gone. Not by accident. Not by chaos. By your will.

This is not just the end of the Rift.

This is the end of everything.

What This Means for the Player
  • You did not just destroy the Rift—you erased it and everything tied to it.
  • Unlike other endings, there is no rebuilding, no future. There is only nothingness.
  • The war, the people, the world itself—wiped away in a single act of absolute finality.
  • This is the true Oblivion Ending—**victory at the cost of existence itself.**

Was this destruction… or was it release?
